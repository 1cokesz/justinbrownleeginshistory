2016: Zero to Hero

In 2016, Justin Brownlee made an unforgettable entrance into the PBA, becoming Barangay Ginebra's import during the Governor’s Cup. His impact was immediate and crucial, as Ginebra sought to end an eight-year title drought. Brownlee quickly established himself as a dominant force on both ends of the floor, but it was in the finals where his legacy was truly cemented.

Facing the Meralco Bolts in a best-of-seven series, the finals were tightly contested, eventually going down to a decisive Game 6. With only a few seconds left on the clock and Ginebra trailing by one point, Brownlee delivered one of the most iconic moments in PBA history: a game-winning, buzzer-beating three-pointer. This shot not only secured the championship for Ginebra but also became the stuff of legends, instantly immortalizing Brownlee in the hearts of Ginebra fans. That 2016 Governors' Cup title marked the beginning of Brownlee’s reign in the PBA and his enduring love affair with Ginebra and its supporters. His composure, versatility, and leadership on the court set the tone for future championships.
